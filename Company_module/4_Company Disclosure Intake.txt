Mandatory Company Disclosure Intake (Backend Source of Truth)
This is everything we must ask the company, even if we don’t show all of it publicly.

1. Legal & Identity
- Certificate of incorporation
- CIN / registration number
- Registered address
- Shareholding structure (current)
- Subsidiaries / group entities
- Jurisdictional exposure (India / overseas)

Admin actions: verify documents, flag inconsistencies

2. Business & Operations
- Detailed business description
- Revenue streams (actual, not planned)
- Key customers (concentration %)
- Supplier dependencies
- Geographic revenue split
- Operating model explanation

Back-and-forth expected: revenue clarity, customer dependency

3. Financial Disclosures
- Last 3–5 years audited financials
- Auditor name & remarks
- Monthly burn rate
- Debt details
- Contingent liabilities
- Cash runway estimate

Admin review: red flags, unusual trends

4. Capital Structure & Securities
- Share classes (equity / CCPS / CCD)
- ESOP pool details
- Fully diluted cap table
- Conversion / preference terms
- Lock-ins on existing shareholders

Critical: many disputes originate here

5. Valuation & Pricing
- Last funding round valuation
- Date & investor of last round
- Valuation methodology
- Justification for current price
- Comparison with peers (company’s view)

Admin challenge zone: inflated assumptions

6. Governance & Management
- Founder backgrounds
- Board composition
- Independent directors (if any)
- Related-party transactions
- Past litigation / regulatory issues

7. Risk Declaration (Signed)
- Business risks
- Regulatory risks
- Liquidity risks
- Technology risks
- Promoter risks

Must be explicitly acknowledged

8. Exit & Liquidity Disclosure
- Intended IPO timeline (non-binding)
- Secondary sale history
- Buyback clauses (if any)
- Exit constraints

9. Ongoing Updates
- Material changes commitment
- Quarterly update obligation
- Disclosure responsibility owner
